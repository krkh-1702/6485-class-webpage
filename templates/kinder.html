Which of the two bikes looks more expensive?

Who looks kinder?

These are the questions we humans can answer with reasonably high accuracy just by looking at the objects. Machine learning is already capable of classifying or generating human faces, vehicles, animals, etc., but how much nuance of an abstract concept would it be able to tell? In this research, we explored (1) training a CNN model to classify expensive- and cheap cars, and (2) steering an abstract attribute on GAN-created human- and museum portrait faces.

Can you be kinder?

Illuminating the boundaries of arbitrary commands on AI
